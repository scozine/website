The Changes of the Viola

The viola changed over the years. Some huge changes and some that didn't make much of a differents. From the scroll to the button, every part plays a role.
From the wedsite, Connolly Music by Revelle Team, they state, "Changes in bow strenght, developed by Francois Tourte, and venues led to the final chapters in the history of the viola. Some of the most influential changes to the viola at the turn of the 19th century included: Heavier strings and increased tension resulting from wrapping gut strings with silver or similar metals, which allowed greater projection of sound. The neck was lengthened and titled at a slightly backward angle in relation to the body. The body, bass bar and bridge were fortified for improved strengh." This proves that the viola had lots of changes.

Eighth Note

A eighth note is played for half the beat.
Example: 1 and 2 and 3 and 4.

Sixteenth Note

A sixteenth note is played for half of a eighth note.
